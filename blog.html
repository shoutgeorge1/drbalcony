Overview of New Housing Laws

On October 6, 2025, Governor Newsom signed a package of bills aimed at strengthening tenant protections and improving housing affordability across California. These measures touch on multiple aspects of rental housing, from habitability standards to eviction process changes and technology regulation.

The new laws represent another step in California's ongoing efforts to balance tenant rights, affordability, and fair housing practices. Property owners and managers must understand these changes to ensure compliance and avoid penalties.

AB 628 - Habitability Standards

AB 628 (McKinnor) - Working Appliances Requirement

Effective: January 1, 2026

What it requires: Beginning January 1, 2026, all new, amended, or extended leases must include a working stove and refrigerator for the unit to be considered habitable.

Key Requirements:

  • Landlords must provide working stoves and refrigerators in all rental units
  • Applies to all new leases, lease renewals, and lease amendments
  • Units without working appliances cannot be considered habitable
  • Tenants can withhold rent or pursue legal action for non-compliance

AB 246 - Social Security Protection

AB 246 (Bryan) - Social Security Benefit Protection

Effective: January 1, 2026

What it requires: Protects tenants from eviction if delayed or reduced Social Security benefits impact their ability to pay rent.

Key Requirements:

  • Landlords cannot evict tenants due to delayed Social Security payments
  • Tenants must provide documentation of Social Security delays
  • Protection applies during benefit processing delays
  • Landlords must work with tenants to establish payment plans

For detailed information: View AB 246 Full Text

AB 414 - Electronic Security Deposits

AB 414 (Pellerin) - Electronic Security Deposit Returns

Effective: January 1, 2026

What it requires: Allows landlords to return security deposits electronically when tenants opt for that method.

Key Requirements:

  • Landlords can offer electronic security deposit returns
  • Tenants must explicitly opt-in to electronic returns
  • Same timeline requirements apply (21 days)
  • Electronic returns must include itemized deductions

For detailed information: View AB 414 Full Text

AB 863 - Multilingual Eviction Forms

AB 863 (Kalra) - Multilingual Eviction Summons

Effective: January 1, 2027

What it requires: Requires the Judicial Council to create multilingual eviction summons forms in English, Spanish, Chinese, Tagalog, Vietnamese, and Korean by January 1, 2027.

Key Requirements:

  • Eviction summons must be available in 6 languages
  • Forms must be accessible through court systems
  • Landlords must use appropriate language forms
  • Improves language access for non-English speaking tenants

For detailed information: View AB 863 Full Text

AB 325 - Pricing Algorithm Restrictions

AB 325 (Aguiar-Curry) - Pricing Algorithm Antitrust

Effective: January 1, 2026

What it requires: Expands California's antitrust laws to include pricing algorithms, targeting unlawful rent pricing practices or artificial scarcity driven by automated systems.

Key Requirements:

  • Prohibits use of pricing algorithms for rent collusion
  • Targets artificial scarcity creation through technology
  • Applies to property management software and platforms
  • Enforcement through existing antitrust mechanisms

For detailed information: View AB 325 Full Text

SB 721 - Apartment Inspections (Deadline Approaching)

üö® URGENT: SB 721 Deadline Approaching

Deadline: January 1, 2026 (Approaching Soon)

If you own or operate apartment buildings with three or more units and have balconies, decks, stairways, or other Exterior Elevated Elements (EEEs) six feet or higher, SB 721 requires inspections and testing.

What You Need to Do Now:

  • Confirm whether your buildings fall under SB 721 coverage
  • Schedule inspections with licensed professionals immediately
  • Complete testing and documentation requirements
  • Address any findings promptly to avoid escalation

Coverage Requirements:

  • Buildings with 3+ units
  • Exterior Elevated Elements (EEEs) 6+ feet high
  • Includes balconies, decks, stairways, walkways
  • Must be inspected by licensed professionals
